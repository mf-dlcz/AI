# Understanding Business Problems
The **SMART** method gives you a structured way to break down complex problems into manageable parts.

<ins>**Specific:**<ins>
- You must pinpoint the exact issue affecting your business. Ask yourself these questions:
    - What is happening?
    - Who is involved?

<ins>**Measurable:**<ins>
- Define your problem with numbers and data. This helps you track progress and success. Consider these 
questions:
    - How many customers are affected?
    - What is the current performance level?
    - What are the costs involved?

<ins>**Achievable:**<ins>
- Your problem definition must consider available resources and realistic solutions. Ask yourself these 
questions:
    - What resources do you have?
    - What obstacles might you face?
    - Who needs to support this initiative?

<ins>**Relevant:**<ins>
- The problem must align with your company's goals and priorities. Consider these points:
    - Does solving this problem support your business strategy?
    - Will fixing this issue improve guest satisfaction?
    - Is this the right time to address this problem?

<ins>**Time-Bound:**<ins>
- Set clear timeframes for addressing the problem. This creates urgency and helps track progress. Include 
the following:
    - When did the problem start?
    - When do you need to solve it?
    - What are the key milestones?
